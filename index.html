The Problem

Why Hand Tremors Are So Debilitating

For the 10 million+ people living with Parkinson's worldwide, hand tremors undermine independence and make daily life a constant challenge.

01
🍽️

Self-Care Difficulties

Patients struggle with essential daily activities such as writing, eating, holding objects, or dressing due to involuntary hand shaking that cannot be consciously controlled.

02
🙋

Loss of Independence

Parkinson's forces many to rely on constant supervision from caregivers, eroding confidence and happiness for those who deeply value their autonomy.

03
🏥

No Feedback, No Data

Clinicians rarely have objective, longitudinal data about tremor severity and patterns, making it harder to fine-tune medication and treatment plans effectively.

Solution

Our Approach: Mechanical First. Intelligent Second.

A cable-driven exoskeleton physically stabilises the finger, while an independent monitoring system captures tremor data.

⚙️

Cable-Driven Stabilisation

The exoskeleton physically guides the fingers, resisting involuntary motion. A cable and pulley system anchors the structure and distributes tension across the joint for tremor resistance.

📡

Monitoring Prototype

A separate IMU sensor on the back of the hand tracks tremor motion and frequency. This monitor also works as a standalone symptom-tracking tool if desired.

Engineering Design

How the Mechanism Works

Every design choice was made to maximise tremor suppression while keeping the device comfortable enough to wear through daily life.

01

Why the PIP Joint?

The proximal interphalangeal (PIP) joint is the primary contributor to functional finger movement. It also offers the largest range of flexion in the finger. Stabilising it has the greatest impact on reducing tremor interference.

02

Why Cable-Driven?

Cable-driven mechanisms are lightweight, efficient, and exceptional at mimicking natural finger movement. This approach keeps the on-hand structure comfortable and unobtrusive, while creating a flexible platform for future integration of motorised actuation and adaptive sensors.

03

The Sector Wheel Mechanism

At the PIP joint, a sector wheel connects to two cables: one governs flexion, the other extension, which allows the device to resist unwanted motion in both directions simultaneously. This is essential for countering the oscillatory nature of Parkinson's tremors.

Data & Monitoring

Why Monitoring Changes Everything

Monitoring elevates the device from a simple wearable to a long-term healthcare tool, providing valuable data even while tremors are suppressed. *Not all features listed have been implemented in the prototype.

📈
Personal Trend Tracking

Long-Term Data Collection

When amplitude is dampened, changes over time are proportional and meaningful, capturing how symptoms evolve over time. Data collected allows doctors to reliably assess progression and adjust treatment with greater precision.

🔁
Early Insights

Early Detection

Shifts in tremor patterns can indicate symptom worsening before it becomes clinically apparent, enabling earlier, more targeted interventions.

🔌
Versatility

Standalone Use

Because the two systems are decoupled in this prototype, the monitoring unit functions independently. Patients in early stages can use it for symptom tracking before mechanical assistance becomes necessary.

🚨
Feedback

Safety Alerts

Unusual tremor events or instability patterns can trigger alerts, helping caregivers respond quickly to potential fall risks or symptom spikes.

🔬
Long-Term

Research Support

Anonymised data contributes to broader Parkinson's research and the development of next-generation assistive technologies.

Why This Approach

Built for Real-World Use

Compared with complex or expensive systems, this project prioritises practicality and daily accessibility without compromising functionality.

🪶

Lightweight

Cable-driven design keeps actuator mass away from the hand. The on-hand structure stays light and unobtrusive.

💶

Affordable

Designed with cost-conscious materials and manufacturing so that more patients can access tremor support.

🔌

Modular Systems

Mechanical and sensing systems can be independent, enabling standalone monitoring use today, and flexible future integration as both components mature.

🔧

Adjustable

The structure adapts to different hand sizes and tremor severity levels, making it suitable for a wide spectrum of Parkinson's patients.

What's Next

Roadmap to a Full Assistive System

The current prototype demonstrates the core concept on a single finger. The path forward scales this into a fully integrated assistive device with motor actuation, wireless monitoring, and adaptive intelligence.

1

Full-Hand Motor Actuation

Expand from one finger to a full-hand system using lightweight durable materials and motor-driven actuation with a compact battery for independent daily operation.

2

Wireless Monitoring & Alerts

Advanced microcontroller with Bluetooth/WiFi for real-time data transmission, onboard storage, and caregiver alerts when abnormal tremors or fall risks are detected.

3

Adaptive Machine Learning

ML algorithms analyse motion patterns to identify individual tremor signatures, predict risk events, and continuously adapt the assistance level to each patient over time.

4

Integrated Sensing + Actuation

Merge the currently separate systems — allowing cable tension to feed directly into monitoring, measuring how hard the device is working as a real-time proxy for underlying tremor severity.
